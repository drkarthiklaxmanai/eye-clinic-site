Computer vision syndrome, also called digital eye strain, refers to a cluster of symptoms -- eye strain, headaches, blurred vision, and dry eyes -- that develop from extended screen use. It's not a single underlying disease but rather a predictable consequence of how the eyes are used during prolonged near-focused, screen-based work.

The good news is that computer vision syndrome responds well to relatively simple interventions: correcting any underlying refractive error, adjusting screen setup and lighting, building in regular visual breaks, and managing the dryness that often accompanies reduced blinking during screen use. A comprehensive eye checkup can identify which of these factors is contributing most in your specific case.
