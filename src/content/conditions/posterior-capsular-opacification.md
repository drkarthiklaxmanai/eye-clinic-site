Posterior capsular opacification occurs when the thin membrane (capsule) left behind after cataract surgery -- which holds the new artificial lens in place -- gradually becomes cloudy. This is not a new cataract, since the original clouded lens was already removed; it's a separate, very common change that can occur months to years after otherwise successful surgery.

The good news is that it's treated with a quick, painless YAG laser capsulotomy performed in a single outpatient visit, with no incision and no recovery time. Most patients notice clearer vision within a day.
