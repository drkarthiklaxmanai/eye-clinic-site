In recent years, many people deferred routine, non-urgent healthcare visits -- including annual eye examinations. As life has returned to a steadier rhythm, we're seeing a renewed focus on catching up on preventive eye care, and for good reason.

## Why deferred checkups matter
Several common eye conditions -- glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, and early cataracts among them -- progress gradually and without obvious symptoms in their early stages. A gap of two or three years without a comprehensive eye exam can mean a condition progresses further than it otherwise would have, simply because it went undetected for longer.

## Increased screen dependency changed the picture too
The shift toward remote work and online learning increased average daily screen time substantially for many people, making digital eye strain and dry eye more prevalent -- another reason a fresh evaluation is worthwhile if it's been a while.

## What a comprehensive eye exam actually checks
Beyond just updating your glasses prescription, a thorough exam includes intraocular pressure measurement (relevant for glaucoma), a dilated retinal examination, and an assessment of overall eye health -- catching issues well before they would otherwise become noticeable.

If your last comprehensive eye exam was more than a year or two ago, now is a good time to schedule one -- regardless of whether your vision currently feels fine.
