Neuro-ophthalmology covers vision problems that originate in the optic nerve or the brain's visual pathways, rather than the eye itself. This includes sudden or unexplained vision loss, double vision that doesn't resolve, visual field loss, unexplained headaches with visual disturbance, and eye findings linked to known neurological conditions such as multiple sclerosis or a pituitary tumor.

Dr. Rajeswari holds a FAICO fellowship in Neuro-ophthalmology, the specialized training needed to evaluate vision problems that don't fit a typical eye diagnosis. Vision changes from neuro-ophthalmic conditions can be an early warning sign of a broader neurological issue, so assessment includes detailed visual field testing, optic nerve evaluation, and coordination with neurology where needed -- timely evaluation can both protect vision and help catch conditions elsewhere in the body.
