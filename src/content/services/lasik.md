LASIK (Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis) reshapes the cornea using a precisely controlled laser, correcting how light focuses onto the retina. It's used to treat myopia (nearsightedness), hyperopia (farsightedness), and astigmatism -- the procedure itself takes only minutes per eye.

Not everyone is a good candidate for LASIK, and a responsible evaluation is the most important step in the entire process. Before any procedure is recommended, Dr. Rajeswari performs detailed corneal mapping and a comprehensive eye health assessment -- if LASIK isn't right for you, you'll be told clearly, along with what alternatives may suit you better.
