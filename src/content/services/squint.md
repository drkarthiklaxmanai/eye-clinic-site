Squint (strabismus) is a misalignment of the eyes that can affect appearance, depth perception, and -- in children -- normal visual development. In children, untreated squint can lead to amblyopia ("lazy eye"), a permanent reduction in vision in the misaligned eye, because the brain learns to ignore its input during critical developmental years.

Evaluation includes assessing the type and degree of misalignment, checking for any underlying refractive error, and determining whether amblyopia is present. Treatment is matched to the specific cause -- not every squint requires surgery, and many cases improve significantly with glasses or vision therapy alone.
